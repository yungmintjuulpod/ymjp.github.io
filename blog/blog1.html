DEA MOVES TO SCHEDULE 7-OH, MGM-15, MP

JULY 18, 2026

The Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) moved to temporarily schedule 7-hydroxymitragynine, Mitragynine-Pseudoindoxyl, and MGM-15 on July 1st. The DEA website sites concerns of addiction and abuse potential for banning these substances. This article aims to explain the effects, benefits, and side effects of using these products*.

What is 7-Hydroxymitragynine?

7-Hydroxymitragynine (also known as 7-oh, 7hydroxy, 7ohmz) is an atypical partial opioid agonist found in Kratom (Mitragyna Speciosa.) Now you might be thinking "what the fuck is an atypical partial opioid agonist? Well let me break that down for you. Atypical means that it is not a normal drug for its class. This doesn't necessarily mean different effects it means that it just isn't chemically like commonly known opioids like morphine or codeine which come from opium. The next part is partial agonist. This means that it activates the opioid receptor to a certain threshold and then it just kind of stops. This is similar to the known opioid suboxone and means that overdose risk is very low because it doesn't let your receptors reach 100% and shut down like morphine or heroin can. 7-oh is found to reach closer to 40-70% receptor saturation which means you cannout overdose on 7-oh alone. You can throw up for a few hours and it sucks but real deal emergency room? highly unlikely. The other part people like to claim about 7-oh is that it is a g-protein biased opioid. This was found to not have a very drastic effect on wether or not an opioid is lethal in recent studies.

OK, so what's the big deal?

Well, 7-oh isn't all sunshine and rainbows like most people say. 7-oh, while only activating your opioid receptors to a certain extent, is found to be about 7-13x stronger than morphine. This is a little nuanced but ill get in to that in a second. 7-ohs high potency unfortunately leads to a decently high addiction potential. Even though you don't really get more out of it at a certain dose it's pretty easy to reach that dose because of it's partial agonism. This combined with it's high potency means that you can keep dosing once a day for a week and double it each day until you reach the ceiling and wake up at the end of the week in withdrawal. This is easier to manage for pain patients as people with less severe pain typically don't need 100% receptor activation unless they have been on opioids for a decade. The 7-oh withdrawal is very intense because of this high potency and is compared to that of heroin. Now I will get in to why 7-ohs potency is overhyped. 7-oh, while being 13x stronger than morphine still is a partial agonist. This means that in order to get an effect out of 7-oh users typically need to take doses similar to that of normal oxycodone for a similar effect. When people say 13x stronger than morphine that means it techically has the binding power of that not the actual effect this is a drug that is commonly dosed 40+mgs if you did that with a full agonist at 13x morphine you would die unless you already have an insane tolerance.

7-oh doesn't seem too bad, what about the other ones?

Well, these other molecules are a bit more nuanced than 7-oh with varying capability and use cases. The first one is Mitragynine-Pseudoindoxyl (MP.) MP is a metabolite of 7-oh and is considered a full opioid agonist with little to zero recreational effect. MP is commonly disliked among the MAT side of the 7-oh community as it has more pain relieving effects through its dual agonism. The second molecule is MGM-15 which is actually part of a family that also has MGM-9 and MGM-16. These are modified 7-oh molecules that were discovered in 2008 in Japan and designed to be longer acting or more potent than 7-OH. MGM-15 is commonly regarded to be 2-3x stronger than 7oh and offers a 12 hour half-life which means it lasts in your system 6x longer than 7-oh does at only a 2 hour half-life. MGM-15 is considered to be useful by most people in the 7-oh community from pain patients to MAT patients. MGM-15 is also a partial agonist but it works on the kappa and delta receptors which means it is like a jack of all trades in the opioid industry. Its partial agonism helps people get off stronger opioids like fentanyl and other RCS and its 12 hour half-life means that you get the benefit of twice a day dosing which is similar to suboxone dosing regimens. MGM-16 was found to be more effective for neuropathic pain than gabapentin and in my experience this applies to MGM-15 to a lesser extent. MGM-15 is very promising for pain because of its triple action, long half life, low risk of death or sedation, and neuropathic pain properties. These benifits don't come completely without risks, though, MGM-15 is anywhere from 10-30x as potent as morphine depending on several factors which means it has about double the addiction risk of 7-oh. This is minor compared to the current opioid of choice for cancer fentantyl which has about 3-7x the addiction risk of 7-oh.

What's the takeaway here?

I won't tell anyone what to think especially when it concerns a topic as touchy as the opioid epidemic. But from my research and the anecdotes of others I have found that MGM-15 should be kept around even if 7-oh and MP get banned. MGM-15 has a lot of benefits that in my opinion outweigh the addiction potential. Millions of Americans take suboxone daily and have to essentially clear their next hour to make sure it absorbs properly whereas MGM-15 can just be taken orally like any other pill. This saves suboxone users time and teeth problems. MGM-15 could be extremely effective for cancer patients because of its pain reliving properties and lack of severe respiratory risk that comes with the national standard in the US (fentanyl or percocet.)
